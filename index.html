Tautology, satisfiability & equivalence

Every compound proposition falls into one of three categories, based on its truth column:

  • ⊤ Tautology — true in every row (always true). E.g. p ∨ ¬p.
  • ⊥ Unsatisfiable — false in every row (a contradiction). E.g. p ∧ ¬p.
  • Satisfiable — true in at least one row. We use it here for formulas that are true in some rows and false in others.

Key relationships:

  • P is a tautology ⇔ ¬P is unsatisfiable.
  • P is unsatisfiable ⇔ ¬P is a tautology.
  • P ≡ Q (equivalent) ⇔ P ↔ Q is a tautology — i.e. their truth columns match. Equivalent columns share an ≡ A chip.

Asking “is this proposition satisfiable?” is the famous SAT problem in computer science.
